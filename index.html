Timeline

An outline of our progress throughout Summer

  • June 1

    Brainstorming

    We collaborated as a group to design our robot, implementing various sketching techniques to create out of the box ideas.

  • June 10-12

    Software Development

    We determined different stages of the competition and reflected that in our software design to allow for modularity. We also determined the various functions needed, and designed state diagrams for said functions.

  • June 23

    Design Proposal

    Submitted a document containing a full description of our mechanical, electrical, and software systems including calculations, circuit diagrams, and state diagrams.

  • June 29

    Prototyping

    We began prototyping our mechanical systems, including the claw mechanism.

  • July 4-7

    Backbone Development

    In preparation for tape following, we modeled the chassis, wheels, and gears in Onshape, and laser cut the parts in addition to soldering and testing QRD sensors and H-Bridge circuits. We also built the zipline bucket, and soldered the IR detection circuit with it optimized to detect a 1 kHz signal.

  • July 11-14

    Construction and Extremeties Development

    We assembled our first tape following chassis and began tuning PID controls. We also designed and built the winch mechanism that lifts the bucket to the zipline, and designed the retrieval arm.

  • July 15-16

    Software

    Wrote the first draft of the code for all stages of the competition in separate files for ease of debugging. We combined the states into a single document during integration.

  • July 21

    Zipline Completion

    Zipline mechanism fully controlled by switches.

  • July 24-27

    Arms and Eyes

    Constructed full retrieval arm and claw prototype and integrated IR sensing into State 1 code.

  • July 28

    Time Trials

    We showcased our progress to the professors, demonstrating functionality in parts because the majority of our systems were not integrated.

  • July 31

    Retrieval Arm

    Debugged the circuit problems with the arm, allowing for first successful retrieval of agent!

  • August 1

    Integration Complete

    We successfully completed a full run for the first time!

  • August 2-9

    Final Touches

    Tuned Frankenbot’s speed and reliability in preparation for competition.

  • Aug 10
    Competition!
